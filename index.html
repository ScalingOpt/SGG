Abstract

We introduce Score-based Generative Guidance (SGG), a new approach to scaling optimization tasks by leveraging the power of score-based generative models. Unlike traditional optimization methods, SGG integrates generative priors to guide the optimization process, resulting in improved convergence and robustness across a variety of complex tasks, including inverse problems, combinatorial optimization, and neural network training. Our experiments demonstrate that SGG outperforms existing baselines by significant margins, establishing a new state-of-the-art in multiple domains.

Introduction

Scaling optimization to handle large-scale and complex problems remains a critical challenge in machine learning and related fields. Traditional optimization techniques often struggle with high-dimensional spaces and non-convex objectives. In this work, we propose Score-based Generative Guidance (SGG), a framework that combines the strengths of score-based generative models with optimization algorithms to address these challenges. By modeling the score function of the data distribution, SGG provides a powerful prior that guides the optimization process toward high-probability regions, improving both efficiency and solution quality.

Method

The SGG framework operates by integrating score-based generative models into the optimization loop. We first train a score model to estimate the gradient of the log-probability density of the target distribution. This score is then used to guide the optimization process, either through gradient-based updates or sampling-based methods. The key components of SGG include:

  • Score Estimation: Training a neural network to approximate the score function using denoising score matching.
  • Guidance Mechanism: Incorporating the score into optimization algorithms such as SGD or Langevin dynamics.
  • Adaptive Scaling: Dynamically adjusting the influence of the generative prior based on optimization progress.

Conclusion

Score-based Generative Guidance (SGG) represents a significant advancement in scaling optimization tasks. By leveraging generative priors, SGG achieves superior performance and efficiency across diverse applications. We believe SGG opens new avenues for research in optimization and generative modeling, and we provide our code and models to facilitate further exploration.
